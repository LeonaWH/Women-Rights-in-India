The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012

The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012 is a legislation enacted by the Government of India with the aim of protecting children from sexual abuse and exploitation. The Act defines various sexual offences against children and provides for procedures for reporting, investigation, and prosecution of such offences.

The Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes(Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989

The Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, commonly known as the SC/ST Act, is a legislation enacted by the Government of India with the aim of preventing atrocities against members of the Scheduled Castes (SCs) and the Scheduled Tribes (STs), who are historically marginalized and oppressed communities in India.

The Indian Penal Code, 1860

The Indian Penal Code (IPC) is a comprehensive criminal law code enacted by the British colonial government in 1860, which is applicable in India. It is the primary criminal law statute in India and governs the prosecution and punishment of various criminal offences.

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 is a legislation enacted by the Government of India to address the issue of domestic violence against women. It defines domestic violence broadly to include physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse, as well as harassment, stalking, and deprivation of resources. The Act empowers women who are victims of domestic violence to seek protection orders from a Magistrate, which can include protection from further acts of domestic violence, monetary relief, custody of children, and residence orders.

Sexual Harassment of women at workplace prohibition Act 2013

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 is a legislation in India aimed at addressing sexual harassment of women at workplaces. It mandates employers to establish Internal Complaints Committees (ICCs) to receive and address complaints of sexual harassment. The Act aims to create a safe and inclusive work environment for women and ensure timely redressal of sexual harassment complaints.

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

This act provides for the protection of women from domestic violence, including emotional, verbal, and economic abuse. The act can be used to protect a woman's right to her property if she is subjected to economic abuse by her husband or his relatives.
