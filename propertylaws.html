Women's Rights in India

Women have the legal right to own, inherit, and transfer property. There are laws and legal provisions related to women's property rights in India.

The Transfer of Property Act, 1882

This act, namely The Transfer of Property Act, 1882, is a comprehensive legal framework that regulates the transfer of property in India through modes such as sale, gift, and mortgage. It grants women the same rights as men to transfer property and ensures transparent and lawful property transfer procedures.

Hindu Succession Act, 2005

Act of the Parliament of India enacted to amend and codify the law relating to intestate or unwilled succession, among Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs. The Act lays down a uniform and comprehensive system of inheritance and succession into one Act. The Hindu woman's limited estate is abolished by the Act. Any property possessed by a Hindu female is to be held by her absolute property and she is given full power to deal with it and dispose it of by will as she likes.Under the Hindu Succession Act, 2005, females are granted ownership of all property acquired either before or after the signing of the Act, abolishing their "limited owner" status.

Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937

Inheritance for Muslims in India is governed by this Act. It is a short statute with five provisions only. This law deals with marriage, succession, inheritance and charities among Muslims. This legislation that codifies Shariat recognises two kinds of legal heirs- sharers and residuaries. Daughters cannot inherit more than half of what the sons inherit. The estate of a Muslim can only pass to a Muslim. Under Shariat law, only 1/3 of the estate can be willed in favour of anyone.

Section 498A

Deals with the offense of cruelty by husband or his relatives towards a married woman, which includes deprivation of her property.

The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961

This act prohibits the giving or taking of dowry. It also provides for punishment for those who demand or give dowry. The act can be used to protect a woman's right to her property as it prohibits dowry-related demands.

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

This act provides for the protection of women from domestic violence, including emotional, verbal, and economic abuse. The act can be used to protect a woman's right to her property if she is subjected to economic abuse by her husband or his relatives.
