The Muslim Women(Protection of Rights on Divorce)Act, 1986

The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986 is an Indian law that protects the rights of Muslim women going through a divorce. It makes it mandatory for a Muslim man to provide for the maintenance of his divorced wife and establishes a Muslim Women's Board to provide legal aid and counseling services. The law has been both praised and criticized for its impact on Muslim women's rights in India.

The Muslim Women(Protection of Rights on Marriage)Act, 2019

The Muslim Women(Protection of Rights on Marriage)Act, 2019 is an Indian law that criminalizes the practice of instant divorce or "triple talaq" in the Muslim community. The law provides for a jail term for up to three years for any Muslim man who pronounces instant divorce on his wife. The Act has been introduced to protect the rights of Muslim women and ensure gender equality in marriage and divorce in India.

The Haryana Compulsory Registration of Marriages Act, 2008

The Haryana Compulsory Registration of Marriages Act, 2008 is a law that mandates the registration of marriages in the state of Haryana, India. The law requires all marriages, including those performed in other states and countries, to be registered within a specific time frame. The Act aims to prevent child marriages, provide proof of marriage, and enable women to claim their rights in case of marital disputes.

The Odisha Muhammedan Marriages and Divorces Registration Act, 1949

The Odisha Muhammedan Marriages and Divorces Registration Act, 1949 is an Indian law that provides for the registration of marriages and divorces among the Muslim community in the state of Odisha. The law requires all Muslim marriages and divorces to be registered within a specific time frame, and also provides for the establishment of a register for the recording of such events. The Act aims to promote social reform, prevent fraud, and provide legal protection to Muslim women in matters of marriage and divorce.

The Rajasthan State Commission for Women Act, 1999

The Rajasthan State Commission for Women Act, 1999 is a law that established a State Commission for Women in the Indian state of Rajasthan. The Commission is responsible for investigating and addressing issues related to the rights and welfare of women in the state, including cases of discrimination, harassment, and violence. The Act provides for the appointment of a Chairperson and members of the Commission, and also outlines the powers and functions of the Commission.

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

This act provides for the protection of women from domestic violence, including emotional, verbal, and economic abuse. The act can be used to protect a woman's right to her property if she is subjected to economic abuse by her husband or his relatives.
